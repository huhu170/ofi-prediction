## 3）主题笔记（Topic Notes）
### OFI/Order Book Imbalance Definitions & Construction
依据E3/E5: The paper defines order flow imbalance via QR (length), HR (height), and LR(q) (liquidity) across multiple book steps (E3). Liquidity imbalance for larger trades (up to5x average) correlates with higher return prediction R² (E5), indicating deeper book info is valuable for short-term prediction.

### Contribution of Deep Order Book Info to Price Discovery
依据E1/E4: The order book beyond the first step contributes ~30% to price discovery (E1). WP¹ (step1) and WP¹⁻¹⁰ (steps1-10) are cointegrated (E4), meaning deep book info reflects long-run equilibrium with shallow info (but adds incremental value).

### Order Book Imbalance & Short-Term Return Prediction
依据E2/E5: Including steps2-10 improves return prediction R² by ~11% (E2). Liquidity imbalance for larger trades (up to5x average) increases R² from6.68% to9.98% (E5), showing deeper book imbalance is more predictive for larger trade sizes.

### Traders' Use of Deep Order Book Info
依据E7: Traders use order book info beyond the first step for order submission decisions (E7), as evidenced by significant coefficients in the ordered probit model (variables for steps2-10 are impactful).
