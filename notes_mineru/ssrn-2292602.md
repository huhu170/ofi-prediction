## 3）主题笔记（Topic Notes）
### OFI/Order Flow Imbalance Definition and Classification
依据证据 E1、E3、E5。The paper defines order flow imbalance via three classification schemes: ACT^R (BBO-based aggressor trade identification, 99.95% accurate), TIC^R (tick rule), and BVC (price change normalized by volatility). VPIN is a rolling average of absolute order imbalances over volume buckets. Actual imbalance (ACT^R) is negatively correlated with volatility, while BVC-based imbalance is positively correlated with volume/volatility.

### VPIN Construction and Volume Regularization
依据证据 E2、E3、E5。VPIN uses volume-synchronized buckets; to address long-term volume trends, the paper applies detrended V (1/50th of preceding month's daily volume). Different classification schemes lead to divergent VPIN behavior: ACT^R VPIN is stable across market conditions, while BVC VPIN fluctuates with volume and volatility.

### Predictive Power of VPIN for Volatility
依据证据 E6、E7、E8。OLS regressions test VPIN's forecast power for 5-minute and 1-day volatility. BVC VPIN has limited predictive power (R² ~10% for 5-minute horizon), which is fully subsumed by realized volatility (RV). During the flash crash, BVC VPIN rises after the crash starts, providing no predictive signal.

### VPIN Behavior During Extreme Events
依据证据 E8、E9。During the flash crash, actual VPIN remains flat, while BVC VPIN rises sharply due to misclassification (correlated with volume/volatility). Random VPIN (RND^T) shows similar behavior to BVC VPIN, indicating no incremental information from BVC classification. The inverse correlation between actual VPIN and volatility is linked to trade size and direction clustering, but root causes (e.g., HFT impact) are not fully verified.
