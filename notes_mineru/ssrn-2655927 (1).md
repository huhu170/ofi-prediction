## 3）主题笔记（Topic Notes）
### HFT Classification and Core Characteristics
依据E1、E2：HFT is defined by volume and inventory criteria, with higher message/trade ratio (64.74 vs non-HFT's27.01) and lower average trade size ($5,663 vs non-HFT's$37,532)

### Order Aggressiveness and Price Discovery Contribution
依据E3、E4、E7：HFTs submit more aggressive orders (improving, NBBO) and their improving orders contribute twice as much to price discovery as non-HFTs

### Volatility Impact on HFT Order Behavior
依据E8、E9：HFTs reduce limit order submissions with higher volatility, while non-HFTs increase them, aligning with GPR's model of speculators switching to market orders

### Public Information's Role in HFT Price Impact
依据E6：HFTs' higher price impact is partially explained by public information (volatility, limit order book imbalance), accounting for ~1/3 of the difference

### Cross-Exchange Price Discovery Leadership
依据E11、E12：HFTs maintain higher info share even at1-second frequency and lead cross-exchange price change sequences

## 4）可直接写进论文的句子草稿（可选）
1. HFTs are classified using volume and inventory criteria, capturing their short-term speculative nature (E1).
2. HFTs' improving limit orders contribute 19.6% to price discovery, significantly more than non-HFTs' 8.2% (E7).
3. HFTs reduce their limit order submissions by3.40% per1% increase in lagged volatility, reflecting risk-averse behavior (E8).
4. Even at the1-second frequency, HFTs maintain a higher minimum information share (59%) than non-HFTs (41%) (E11).
5. The higher price impact of HFTs' trades is partially explained by public information like lagged index returns and limit order book imbalance (E6).
6. HFTs initiate and close more cross-exchange price change sequences than non-HFTs, demonstrating their role in market integration (E12).
