## 3）主题笔记（Topic Notes）
### Order Flow Imbalance (Y_t) Definition and Dynamics
依据证据E1,E2：Y_t is a mean-reverting state variable capturing supply-demand fluctuations from buyer/seller-initiated trades. Its dynamics include execution rate (ẋ_t) via information leakage term φ(ẋ_t), modeling how trader actions impact market expectations of future order flow.

### Myopic vs Dynamic Execution Strategies
依据证据E3,E4：Myopic strategies (VWAP/ML) are fixed-horizon and deterministic, ignoring Y_t. Dynamic strategies (DH) adapt to real-time Y_t and inventory x_t to compute optimal execution rates, leading to lower costs.

### Optimizing Execution Horizon
依据证据E6,E7：Endogenous execution horizon (optimized T) reduces costs by ~6.8% vs fixed T. Dynamic strategies adjust T based on current Y_t and x_t, leading to shorter horizons in unbalanced markets and longer in balanced ones.

### Data and Empirical Validation
依据证据E5：Paper uses order book data from US exchanges (NASDAQ, BATS) for equities (MSFT, TEVA, BBBY) to calibrate models. Data includes executed trades and limit orders at the touch (best bid/ask).

### Limitations of the Model
依据证据E8,E9：Model is restricted to pure selling strategies (no buying) and lacks price risk. Exact mapping from observed order imbalance (I_k) to model Y_t remains an open empirical question.
